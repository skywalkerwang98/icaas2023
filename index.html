Introduction

Addressing the crucial need for a holistic integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI), architectural innovations, and advanced security solutions, we advocate for a collaborative approach to foster a smarter, safer future. It is a well-recognized fact that AI alone, while powerful, is not inherently capable of intuitively adapting to changes or making predictions without significant data input. Thus, an effective combination of AI with innovative architectural designs and state-of-the-art security solutions can bring about significant improvements in efficiency, safety, and user experience. Ideally, AI systems should be designed and trained based on principles that align with the realities of architectural design and security to ensure their relevance and applicability in various scenarios.

Moreover, the scope of architectural innovations and security solutions should not be limited to their traditional domains. The integration of AI, architecture, and security should lead to modular systems where knowledge about the world is decomposed into independent and recomposable pieces, contributing to the creation of systems that behave in a causally sound manner. Such systems would be more robust and flexible, offering a strong foundation for future advancements in these intersecting fields.

The objective of this conference is to cultivate such an integration, grounded in interdisciplinary collaboration and innovation. We argue that the expertise associated with the AI, architecture, and security community, be it theoretical or practical, naturally aligns with the demands of modern society, and could therefore form a robust framework for a smarter, safer future. As an illustration, in the evolving smart city projects (http://smartcityprojects.com), traditional architectural and security solutions struggle to keep pace with rapidly changing urban dynamics. An AI-driven, innovative architectural and security framework could provide a dependable technological seed upon which future cities could grow and thrive. Exploring this integrated approach from various angles is the central theme of this conference.

Call For Papers

Topics

We invite the global research community to submit original work on the proposed fusion of artificial intelligence, architecture, and security, focusing on, but not restricted to, the following topic areas:

  • Machine Learning and its real-world applications in architecture and security
  • Challenges in integrating Deep Learning with architecture and security systems
  • Neuro-symbolic approaches in Natural Language Processing for secure and smart architecture
  • Probabilistic world models for Computer Vision in architecture and security
  • Probabilistic inference for robotics in architectural and security contexts
  • Datasets for Knowledge Representation and Reasoning in architecture and security
  • Metrics for assessing Cognitive Computing methodologies in the context of architecture and security
  • Limitations and potential of Machine Learning in Explainable AI for architecture and security: How far can we go?
  • Interrelation between ethical reasoning and spatio-temporal reasoning in AI, architecture, and security

While the list above is comprehensive, it is not exhaustive. The goal is to stimulate discourse on all aspects of the proposed integration of artificial intelligence, architecture, and security.

Submission

Guidelines

Papers should be formatted according to the ACM conference formatting guidelines and submitted as a single PDF file. We welcome submissions across the full spectrum of theoretical and practical work including research ideas, methods, tools, simulations, applications or demos, practical evaluations, and surveys. Submissions that are 2 pages long (excluding references and appendices) will be considered for a poster, and submissions that are at least 4 pages and up to 6 pages long (excluding references and appendices) will be considered for an oral presentation. All papers will be peer-reviewed in a single-blind process and assessed based on their novelty, technical quality, potential impact, clarity, and reproducibility (when applicable). Workshop submissions will be handled by EasyChair; the submission link is as follows: https://easychair.org/conferences/?conf=icaas2023

All questions about submissions should be emailed to icaas2023 at easychair.org.
